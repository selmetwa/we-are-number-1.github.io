Country Correlation Map

Select a country from the dropdown menu to see which other countries are closely related. The map will recolor itself to display the most similar countries and the least similar countries.

The correlation was done by creating an array filled with the percentile scores of each metric for every country, and then comparing every array to the target country using the Pearson Correlation Coefficient (PCC). The numbers you see when hovering over a country represents each countries score from the PCC.

Key takeaways from this map:

Most countries are similar to nearby countries

A country like Egypt is closely correlated to most of Africa and the rest of the Arab world. The same trend applies to Western Europe, most of South America, and most of Asia.

Some countries are not similar to nearby countries

Countries like Japan and Korea and more related to Western Europe & North America than China, and Southeast Asia. Also Uruguay is unlike most of South America and again resembles Western Europe more than neighboring countries.

Some countries are oddly unique

A few countries, most notably, Tunisia and Mongolia, are not closely correlated to any other countries on the map.
